When we stepped out of the carriage, a bridge stood directly before us.
It was an old wooden suspension bridge that looked as if it might rot and collapse at any moment.
＃紅緒
My home is just on the other side.
She spoke as if it were the most natural thing in the world.
＃主人公
The other side, you say...
The suspension bridge creaked and groaned as it swayed.
It wasn't exactly a sheer cliff, but if I slipped and fell, there was no way I'd survive.
＃紅緒
The carriage cannot go any further than this, you see.
Benio-san checked the luggage the coachman had unloaded from the carriage.
＃紅緒
Three days from now, then.
＃御者
Yes, ma'am. I'll be bringing the fuel then.
＃紅緒
Right. If anything else is needed, I shall call. I'm counting on you.
＃御者
Understood, Lady Benio.
The coachman bowed and climbed back into the carriage.
＃想子
She's quite something, isn't she?
Souko-san whispered into my ear.
＃主人公
She definitely has the air of a high-born lady.
＃想子
It's like she was born knowing exactly how to command others.
＃主人公
Aren't you pretty much the same, Souko-san?
＃想子
I am most certainly not. Anyway, never mind that—go help her with her bags.
Souko-san poked me in the back, nudging me toward Benio-san.
＃主人公
Ah, Benio-san, would you like me to carry those for you?
＃紅緒
Oh no, I couldn't possibly ask a guest to do such a thing.
＃想子
Please, don't hesitate to use him. His physical stamina is about the only thing he can be proud of.
＃主人公
That's not true at all.
＃紅緒
Hehe... and yet, isn't he already carrying your luggage as well?
＃想子
It's light, so it's fine.
Actually, Souko-san, that cola of yours is incredibly heavy.
＃紅緒
You really don't have to push yourself, you know?
＃主人公
I don't think I'm overdoing it, but...
With my rucksack and Souko-san's on my back, I took Benio-san's bags in both hands.
I could manage to walk, but crossing that suspension bridge like this was a little terrifying.
＃想子
Were you out doing some shopping?
＃紅緒
Yes. Normally our maid handles it, but I had some errands of my own to attend to in the city.
＃想子
It must be difficult, traveling from all the way out here.
＃紅緒
Quite. In the summer, one can drive a car up this far, but once the snow piles up, that's no longer an option.
＃主人公
Can a carriage climb higher than a car?
＃想子
Horses are much better for snowy roads. A car would just slide all over the place.
＃紅緒
Exactly. Shall we proceed, then?
Benio-san began to walk.
I followed behind her, staggering slightly.
As we neared the suspension bridge, the sense of dread only intensified.
＃主人公
...It would be a disaster if someone fell, wouldn't it?
＃想子
Is there no other way in?
＃紅緒
There is none. If this bridge were to be destroyed, we would be completely isolated.
Benio-san gave a melodic little laugh.
＃主人公
I really don't think that's something to laugh about.
I carefully watched my step as I slowly made my way across the bridge.
＃主人公
Souko-san.
I called out to Souko-san behind me.
＃想子
What is it?
＃主人公
Don't even think about pushing me from behind, even as a joke.
＃想子
I won't. It would be a catastrophe if my cola fell.
I wish she'd worry about me just a little bit.
＃想子
The wind is so strong...
The snow was blowing almost horizontally.
＃紅緒
Once we cross the bridge and pass through the woods, we'll be right there. Please bear with it for a moment longer.
＃主人公
I'm fine, but...
I wondered if Souko-san's hand was alright.
Having finished crossing the suspension bridge, I let out a sigh of relief.
A line of trees stood ahead of us, with a narrow path winding between them.
I still couldn't see any buildings from here.
＃想子
Is the place up ahead the 'Nanatsuki-kan'?
＃紅緒
That seems to be what it is called in the village.
＃主人公
Nanatsuki-kan?
＃想子
Apparently, it's not written with the character for 'moon,' but with the one for 'haunt' or 'possess.'
＃紅緒
The Nanatsuki family originally used that character, I am told. Though it was changed several generations ago because the name was considered quite unlucky.
＃主人公
Well, the moon in the sky is certainly much prettier.
＃紅緒
Indeed it is.
Once we entered the woods, the wind and snow seemed to calm down somewhat.
＃紅緒
Are you familiar with the appearance of the Nanatsuki-kan?
＃想子
I've heard stories. Something about it being built by an architect from abroad.
＃紅緒
It was apparently built shortly after the start of the Meiji era. At the time, there were no other Western-style mansions of this scale in the Tohoku region.
Benio-san began her explanation as she walked slowly.
＃紅緒
It is said that the head of the family at the time was so enamored with a building he saw overseas that he had a replica built here.
＃主人公
What does it look like?
＃紅緒
Well, it would be best for you to see it for yourselves.
Benio-san raised her arm and pointed ahead.
Through a break in the trees, something like a black mountain appeared.
It was a building whose outer walls were painted jet black.
Its overwhelming presence was such that one could never lose sight of it, no matter how the snow raged.
It looked like a void of darkness, as if a piece of the world had been hollowed out right there.
A large clock was mounted high above the entrance.
＃主人公
So this is the Nanatsuki-kan...
＃紅緒
It is quite unusual, is it not? Even now, many strange artifacts remain within.
＃想子
I wonder if those are what gave rise to the legends.
＃紅緒
Perhaps. If the opportunity arises, I shall tell you all about them.
Benio-san wore an indescribable smile.
＃紅緒
But before that, we must see to your exhaustion. And it seems Kirihara-san is injured as well.
＃想子
You noticed?
Souko-san seemed a little surprised.
＃紅緒
I noticed you were shielding your hand for some time now. Our family doctor is in residence here, so it would be best to have him examine it later.
＃想子
I see. Thank you.
＃紅緒
Now then, welcome... to the Nanatsuki-kan.
Benio-san gave a graceful bow, gesturing toward the jet-black mansion towering before us.
